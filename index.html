Rock&Roll UpsideDown — Co-Ownership Agreement

This agreement establishes the terms under which Wolf Schram ("Co-Owner A") and David Lemmink ("Co-Owner B") jointly own and operate the Rock&Roll UpsideDown platform and all intellectual property created within the repository rock-and-roll-upside-down.

1. Joint Ownership

All intellectual property, code, concepts, designs, business methodologies, and innovations created within this repository or arising from collaboration on this project are jointly owned 50/50 by both co-owners, regardless of who originated the idea or wrote the code.

2. Decision Making

Major architectural decisions, external partnerships, licensing agreements, fundraising activities, and any action that materially affects the platform require the written consent of both co-owners. Day-to-day development decisions within sandbox environments require no approval.

3. Revenue & Expenses

All revenue generated by the platform shall be split 50/50 after agreed operating costs. Both co-owners must approve any expenditure exceeding $500 USD. Operating costs shall be documented and agreed upon in writing before being incurred.

4. Contributions

Git commit history serves as the authoritative record of individual contributions. Each co-owner is expected to contribute in good faith. No minimum contribution requirement is imposed during the development phase.

5. Dissolution

This partnership may only be dissolved by mutual written agreement of both co-owners, or through binding arbitration under California law if mutual agreement cannot be reached. Upon dissolution, IP rights and any platform value shall be divided 50/50 or as otherwise agreed in writing.

6. Confidentiality

Both co-owners agree to maintain strict confidentiality regarding platform architecture, business strategy, and any non-public information shared in the course of this collaboration, both during and for a period of 3 years following any dissolution.

7. Sandbox Environments

Each co-owner maintains an individual sandbox environment (sandbox/wolf, sandbox/dave) for experimental development. Work in sandboxes is the property of the individual until merged into the develop or main branch, at which point it becomes jointly owned.

8. Governing Law

This agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California, USA, without regard to conflict of law principles.
